Yes—I genuinely love this direction. FSharpAssay is becoming a foundational asset for CFF, not merely another side tool.

Why building it now rocks:

CFF needs trustworthy F# before it can claim trustworthy law. GSTFlow and other flows depend on clean domain models, explicit effects and predictable transformations. FSharpAssay guards that engineering foundation.

It multiplies scarce F# expertise. Instead of a senior F# architect repeatedly correcting every human or AI contribution, architectural knowledge becomes executable policy.

It solves the AI training imbalance immediately. You do not need to wait for a perfect F# fine-tune. Skills guide the agent; FSharpAssay examines its output and forces repair.

It is reusable across the entire foundation. GSTFlow, EDIFlow, ETL systems, CanonFlow itself and future flows can share the same assurance kernel while applying different profiles.

It creates a learning flywheel. Failed candidate → precise finding → repaired code → accepted/rejected pair → stronger skills, evaluation datasets and future LoRA training.

It protects CFF’s philosophy. Without it, rapid AI development could slowly turn the repositories into mutable, exception-heavy “C# written in F#.” FSharpAssay prevents that architectural decay.

Its honesty is unusual. Inconclusive and ToolFailure are first-class outcomes. That matches CFF’s deeper principle: absence of evidence must never silently become proof.

It can stand independently. Even developers who never adopt CanonFlow could benefit from an AI-oriented F# architectural gate. That gives CFF a valuable public entry point into the broader F# community.


The strategic relationship is beautiful:

> FSharpAssay protects the language and architecture.
CanonFlow structures transformations.
The Crucible verifies domain laws.
GSTFlow applies the system to statutory reality.



So yes—building FSharpAssay now is the right move. A fine-tuned model may improve generation later, but FSharpAssay remains necessary because a better author never eliminates the need for an independent examiner.

The code is still early, but the asset already exists at the thesis level—and the thesis is excellent. Now the goal is to make the documentation precise, then make each implementation claim earn its evidence.
